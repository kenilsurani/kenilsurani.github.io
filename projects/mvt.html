Manual Variable Transmission (MVT) for Electric Vehicle

Academic Project (2021–2022)
Team: Kenilkumar Surani, Harshal Vaholiya, Kishan Viradiya
Funding: Student Startup and Innovation Policy (SSIP) 2021

Project Description

Developed an innovative Manual Variable Transmission (MVT) system specifically designed for electric bikes, at a time when most EV bikes used only single-speed direct-drive mechanisms. The introduction of a multi-ratio transmission aimed to significantly improve vehicle range, efficiency, acceleration, and hill-climbing performance.

Motivation & Innovation

Traditional electric bikes operate efficiently only within a narrow speed band due to single-speed direct-drive systems. By integrating a manually variable transmission, the motor can operate within its optimal efficiency zone across different speeds and loads.

The system architecture included:

  • Two cone pulleys
  • V-belt transmission
  • Power screw actuators
  • Dual BLDC motors controlled via Raspberry Pi

This enabled intelligent gear ratio adjustment according to vehicle speed and load conditions.

Role & Responsibilities

  • Lead CAD designer responsible for all MVT components and assembly in SolidWorks
  • Designed and fabricated a dedicated test bench for transmission validation
  • Supervised CNC manufacturing at university workshop
  • Led system integration and real-world testing in an operational electric bike

Technical Features & Validation

  • Gear ratio range: 3.1 to 1.4
  • Torque & speed variation: 458 – 1021 rpm at constant motor speed
  • Maximum torque output: 91 Nm
  • Vehicle speed range: 17 – 60 km/h
  • Clutchless operation

Experimental Results:

  • Up to 40% higher gradeability than standard direct-drive bikes
  • Up to 3x acceleration improvement
  • 33% reduction in required motor size for equivalent performance
  • 10% increase in vehicle range without battery modification

Key Outcomes

  • SSIP 2021 funding supported prototype development and validation
  • Recognized as a novel drivetrain engineering solution within university innovation programs
  • Successfully deployed and tested on a working electric bike
